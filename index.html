Intro

Do you have a hard time trying to figure out what to wear?
Worry not, DigiCloset will help you!
This project aims to solve a problem faced by individuals who are having a hard time trying to keep track of their outfits.

With this application, users are able to create their outfits and save them. They can modify and label them for use cases and various instances.

This project has been carefully designed to meet the standards set by the studies of Human Computer Interaction. The design follows the principles of usability and accessibiliy.

Design

Original Paper mockup

Our Design process consistented of constant feedback from our target audience to ensure create a product tailored to our audience. Through the use of Mass feedback from our target audience, feedback from peers, and focused test interviews we were able to refine our design and create a product that was tailored to our audience. This iterative process allowed us to identify and address pain points effectively, ensuring a seamless user experience. This feedback helped drastically change our design, tailoring it to audience use cases, as well as ensuring that our design was user friendly and easy to use. The test interviews were key in changes in the button layout, as well as the icons as there were pain points in navigation

Final Design

As you can see many parts changed from the original design to the final. The use of clearer containers to seperate the pages, navigation buttons moved for easier use on a mobile device, as well as a standard settings icon. Many things were kept, but through feedback from our target audience we learned the importance of the item. For example, through our mass online survey we learned that weather was a major factor in outfit choice. We incorporated the Weather on the homepage, as well as recommendations based on the weather. We thought that weather was important, but almost all users said that was a top consideration.

prototypes

Based on our studies of computer human interaction and taking feedback from people from various backgrounds, we have made changes to our design, striving to improve our product's prototype.

Final Prototype

Design 3 shown below was selected as our final paper prototype. Since the app relies on uploading pictures of clothing, a mobile platform makes the most sense. Our primary audience is also accustomed to mobile experiences, making it the most intuitive choice. Additionally, using a mobile app is far more convenient than uploading files to a desktop or navigating a website.

Paper prototype 1

Design 1 covers the mobile app version. Design 1 shows the user outfit suggestions on the main screen. The application detects the weather for the day and gives suggestions of outfits from the user’s wardrobe. Upon pressing the burger icon, the user sees different options that the application offers.



Paper prototype 2

Design 2 covers the desktop version: The user upon entering the website is welcomed with the logo and interesting imagery showcasing the app. Upon logging in, the user can see today’s pick based on the weather. They have a button on the screen which will take them to the closet management screen where the user can edit their outfits by adding and removing items from the saved outfits.



Paper prototype 3

Design 3 covers another Mobile version of the application. The design allows the user to add new clothing items by uploading a picture of the item, with optional items like name, color, weather, description. The user can also create outfits with all their items. They can choose the name of the outfit as well as the category.
